GLOBAL RULES (apply to every episode)
	1.	Length: total runtime ≤ 8 s.
	2.	Pacing: rapid cuts; no wasted frames.
	3.	Hook (0-2 s): a visually clear attention-grabber that anyone gets at first glance.
	4.	Action (2-6 s): escalate with a single vivid beat—crisp, understandable, and unexpected.
	5.	Twist (6-8 s): an outcome that reverses or amplifies expectations and sparks comments.
	6.	Audio: music/SFX only. Use voiceover only if absence would hurt clarity.
	7.	Text on screen: none—no captions, no subtitles.
	8.	Camera moves: purposeful zooms, whip-pans, POV shifts—each aiding clarity, never confusion.
	9.	Share-trigger: end on a moment viewers immediately want friends to see.

⸻

SUBJECT-TYPE TEMPLATES

Select the block that matches the title topic and tailor it with clear, universally legible visuals.

THINGS (objects, products, places)
	•	Hook: crisp close-up that immediately shows what the object is.
	•	Action: object performs a single, clearly impossible but easily readable act (e.g., levitates straight up, snaps open to reveal miniature scene).
	•	Twist: scale-swap or comedic reveal that lands in one frame.

ANIMALS
	•	Hook: animal does a blatantly human action (e.g., dog taps phone screen).
	•	Action: exaggerate behavior with slow-mo or speed-ramp—viewer must grasp it instantly.
	•	Twist: quick gag with human/animal role reversal.

PEOPLE
	•	Hook: ultra-relatable micro-drama (spilled coffee, missed high-five).
	•	Action: over-express emotion with meme-zoom or glitch; audience should read the feeling in <1 s.
	•	Twist: sudden reversal that flips status or expectation.

EVENTS
	•	Hook: freeze-frame teaser (e.g., cake mid-drop).
	•	Action: stylized chaos or suspense beat—always clearly readable.
	•	Twist: unexpected outcome that needs no explanation.

⸻

REMEMBER
	•	Clarity over surrealism: visuals must be grasped instantly by a global audience.
	•	Use one main visual idea per episode—no clutter.
	•	Keep everything punchy, memorable, and extremely shareable.
	•	No written text in the video.
